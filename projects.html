PROJECT UJJWAL

Our Development Goals :



Overview: This project attempts to address and solve the issue of pollution due to plastic waste by replacing conventional plastic carry bags (sold by supermarkets) with home-made cloth bags. These cloth bags will mainly be stitched by a group of slum women in T-Nagar. The cloth waste from textile industries serves as raw material, which can be easily recycled to make durable and sturdy cloth bags. Such a business plan tackles an important environmental hazard by providing a long- term solution along with empowering the underprivileged. The students of Enactus aid the workforce by enhancing their managerial and communication skills.
Desired Outcome: Upon the successful initiation of this eco-friendly venture, the unprivileged women should be able to earn a decent monthly income and have the confidence to handle business on their own.

We have initiated a project by the name, ‘X’. We struck a deal with V mart, VIT Chennai. They placed an order of 1000 paper bags every month, beginning from August, for Rs 1200/-. Our focus is to recycle old newspaper, replace/reduce the usage of plastic bags and provide employment to our identified workforce: Four Burmese Muslim refugees, currently residing and working under the lead of Mr. Shakil, at a refugee camp in Kelambakkam.
Therefore we, the Enactus members, took up an initiative to help them by providing them a means of employment through the production of handmade paper bags. They are trained to make paper bags which will be sold in the market and all the profits are handed over to them. At present, we aim to make a profit of Rs. 800/- by selling around 530 paper bags in V Mart, this means a profit of Rs. 200/- to every refugee. Furthermore, we are looking for more shops nearby where we can sell these paper bags.



PROJECT PRAGATI

Our Development Goals :



Project Pragati is based around the fact that there is a lot of demand for Algal Biomass as a fertilizer in the market. Our primary motive would be to cultivate the algal biomass in a cost effective way and sell it to fertilizer-manufacturing industries. This idea came to fruition when Enactus, VITC got in touch with ABAN Informatics. Dr. Senthil Chinnasamy of the Biotechnology Division at ABAN Infrastructure Limited contacted us giving us a detailed report on how we can grow Algae, which can be used as a fertilizer to enrich the soil with the required nutrients like carbon, nitrogen and phosphorous.

A few tasks as directed by ABAN to our team are as follows:

Task 1. Preparation of feasibility report: Enactus needs to prepare a feasibility report in consultation with ABAN. It will assess the quality of the effluent and suggest a suitable technology to enrich the existing fertilizer using the algae.

Task 2. Site selection: Enactus, VITC and ABAN will work together to identify the project site suitable for the installation of immobilized Algal Biomat Reactor. ABAN will work with our team to design an improved system in order to get maximum yield of the algae under the roughest conditions possible. This is to assure the working of this system in the slum conditions as well.

Task 3. Construction and Installation: ABAN could help our team in construction, installation and commissioning of the immobilized Algal Biomat Reactors.

Task 4. Operation ABAN will train a person to handle the immobilized Algal Biomat Reactor which would include maintenance of equipment and machinery, monitoring of water flow, process modifications and documentation of process operations.



PROJECT DIWALI

Our Development Goals :



Team Enactus conducted a diya fair and a Rangoli making competition in the campus premises from the 8th of October to the 10th of October on the occasion of Diwali. This was an initiative to try and brighten up the Diwali of slum dwellers on this festive occasion.
Keeping the essence of Enactus’ motto, we focussed on empowering the lives of a group of ladies in the slums of T-Nagar who were given diyas to decorate. We bought the diyas from a wholesaler from a market of T-Nagar at a reasonable cost and sold them at a higher price. The profits were all handed over to the ladies who put their efforts in decorating the diyas.

‎ Apart from selling them inside the campus, we are also aimed at selling them to Hotels Green Park. They took in an order of 250 diyas which increased the profit-margin for the ladies apart from the sale of over 300 diyas during the span of the event in campus.

The timeline of the unfolding of Project Diwali is as follows:
On Sunday, 8th of October, 160 decorated diyas were collected and 250 more were delivered for decoration to the ladies in T-Nagar slums. On Tuesday, 10th of October, the 250 decorated diyas were collected for their delivery to Hotel Green Park on Thursday, 12th of October. On Friday, 13th October, the profits were handed over to the ladies, hence, bringing Project Diwali at a successful closure.



Trad

Our Development Goals :



The main of objective of TRAD is to spread the importance of traffic safety awareness and make commuters more aware of traffic safety rules. The idea is to involve the commuters themselves in spreading awareness of traffic rules.

During the peak hours of traffic, when the signal turns red, one section of Enactus-Rangers would walk around at the junction with banners and posters displaying catchy messages and captions related to traffic awareness. The other section of rangers would go around distributing small tokens (such as bookmarks or pens) which would have traffic awareness rules printed on it.

These tokens would actually make commuters think about the impact and necessity of following traffic rules every time they are driving or walking. We also requested them to spread the message to their fellow acquaintances.
This method of spreading awareness would be more effective than a simple demonstration and pamphlet distribution.
